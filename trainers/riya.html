About Riya Desai

Riya Desai is one of ChessWithRahul Academy's newest and most rapidly growing trainers, based in Malad West and covering Goregaon and Kandivali West. In just two years of coaching, she has built a 12-student roster with a 4.8★ rating — driven by a creative, activity-based teaching style that young children absolutely love.

Riya specialises in younger children — particularly ages 5 to 10 — an age range that requires a very specific teaching approach. Standard chess instruction moves too fast and too abstractly for this age group. Riya slows everything down, builds on curiosity and play, and uses physical activity, colouring, and storytelling alongside the chess board to make learning multi-sensory and memorable.

Her weekend batch slots are consistently oversubscribed — families in Malad and Goregaon appreciate the option to schedule chess on Saturday or Sunday mornings, keeping weekday evenings free for homework and other activities. Weekday evening slots are also available for families who prefer that rhythm.

Riya is mentored directly by Rahul and reviewed quarterly to ensure she maintains the academy's teaching quality standard. Her rapid growth in students and ratings in her first two years marks her as one of the most promising coaches in the ChessWithRahul network.

What Riya Teaches

🎨

Activity-Based Learning

Drawing piece movement diagrams, colouring chess boards, physical piece placement activities — chess learning that engages the whole child.

📖

Story Chess

Each piece has a character and role in an ongoing chess story — making openings and tactics emotionally engaging rather than abstract.

🧩

Mini-Puzzles

Simple one and two-move tactical puzzles designed for the 5–10 age group — achievable challenges that build pattern recognition quickly.

🏆

Mini Tournaments

Friendly in-session mini-tournaments with medals and certificates — creating excitement and motivation to improve between sessions.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the youngest age Riya teaches? +
Riya regularly teaches children as young as 5 years old. Her multi-sensory, activity-based approach is specifically designed for this age group. She does not recommend formal chess coaching before age 5.
Are weekend batch slots available with Riya in Malad? +
Yes — weekend sessions (Saturday and Sunday mornings) are Riya's most popular option. These fill up quickly, so book early to secure a weekend slot in Malad or Goregaon.
Does Riya cover Kandivali West as well? +
Yes — Kandivali West is within Riya's coverage zone. She also covers parts of Link Road and Mindspace area. Confirm your exact address when WhatsApping.
How does Riya approach children who have a short attention span? +
Riya's sessions are deliberately structured in 10–15 minute activity blocks rather than one continuous lesson. This is specifically designed for children with short attention spans — each block feels like a new game or challenge.
