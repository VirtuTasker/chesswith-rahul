About Arjun Mehta

Arjun Mehta is ChessWithRahul Academy's competitive chess specialist, based in Borivali West and covering Dahisar, Mira Road, and the northern end of Mumbai's Western Line. A FIDE-rated player himself, Arjun brings the depth of competitive chess experience that casual club coaches simply cannot match.

His primary focus is Elo improvement — taking players who are stuck at a certain rating and systematically dismantling the weaknesses keeping them there. Students working with Arjun consistently gain 200–400 Elo points within 6 months of twice-weekly coaching. His methods are methodical, data-informed, and relentlessly practical.

Arjun's coaching toolkit includes deep opening repertoire construction tailored to each player's style, intensive tactical puzzle training (pattern recognition over calculation), precise endgame technique, and psychological preparation for tournament environments. He reviews actual game files from his students — not generic exercises.

Beyond competitive players, Arjun also works with motivated beginners in Borivali who want to develop a serious foundation from day one — students who want to be competitive eventually, not just casual players. His structured approach produces faster beginners than most coaches because he teaches patterns rather than rules.

Frequently Asked Questions

What Elo range does Arjun specialise in? +
Arjun primarily works with players rated 800–1800. Below 800, he can still help but the coaching focus shifts to fundamentals first. Above 1800, he will assess and advise accordingly.
Does Arjun cover Mira Road for home coaching? +
Yes, Arjun covers Mira Road as an extension of Dahisar. It may have limited slot availability. WhatsApp to confirm before booking.
How does Arjun's game review process work? +
You share your game PGN or Chess.com username. Arjun reviews 3–5 recent games before the session, identifies recurring patterns of loss, and builds the session around those specific issues.
Can Arjun prepare me for a specific tournament? +
Yes. Tournament-specific preparation is one of Arjun's strongest areas — he will research the likely competition style, build specific opening preparation for expected opponents, and work on match-day psychology.
