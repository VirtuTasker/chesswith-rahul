Your Beginner Chess Learning Journey

What to expect week by week in your chess lessons

Week 1-2

Learn the Basics

How pieces move, board setup, basic rules. By end of week 2, you'll understand all chess rules and can play a complete game (even if not perfectly). Foundation established!

Week 3-4

Tactics & Thinking

Learn basic tactical patterns. Start thinking ahead one or two moves. Practice finding forks, pins, simple combinations. Chess starts making sense!

Week 5-6

Strategy & Plans

Opening principles, piece development, center control. Learn to create plans, not just random moves. Play more purposeful chess with clear ideas.

Week 7-8

Confident Play

Combine everything learned. Play complete games confidently. Recognize common patterns. Make good decisions. You're now a chess player, not a beginner!

Beginner Chess Lessons - Common Questions

Everything beginners want to know before starting

I've NEVER played chess. Can I really learn?

Absolutely YES! 90% of my students start with zero chess knowledge. If you can learn anything new in life, you can learn chess. My beginner lessons are designed specifically for people who've never played. I explain everything from scratch in simple language.

How long to learn chess as a beginner?

With weekly beginner lessons, most students play confidently within 4-6 weeks. After 2-3 months, you'll play decent games. Remember: learning chess is a journey, not a destination. You'll keep improving forever!

Am I too old to learn chess?

You're NEVER too old to learn chess! I've taught beginners aged 6 to 72. My oldest beginner student started at 68 and now plays daily. Chess is mental, not physical - age doesn't matter. If you want to learn, you can learn!

Do I need to buy a chess set?

No! I bring professional chess boards, pieces, and all materials to your home for every beginner lesson. You don't need to buy anything to start learning chess. Just show up ready to learn!
