My Teaching Philosophy

How I approach coaching to ensure every student succeeds

Beginner-First Mindset

I specialize in teaching complete beginners because I believe everyone deserves to experience the joy of chess. I start from absolute basics‚Äîhow to set up the board, how pieces move‚Äîand build systematically. No prior knowledge is required, and no question is too basic. My goal is to make chess approachable and remove any intimidation factor.

Understanding Over Memorization

I don't teach chess through rote memorization of moves. Instead, I focus on helping you understand the principles behind good chess. When you understand why a move is strong, you can apply that knowledge in countless positions. This approach creates independent thinkers who can analyze positions on their own, not just followers of memorized sequences.

Patient, Personalized Coaching

Every student learns at their own pace, and I respect that completely. Some grasp concepts quickly, others need more time‚Äîboth are perfectly fine. I tailor my teaching speed, examples, and practice exercises to match each student's learning style and pace. There's never any pressure or judgment. Your progress is measured against your own starting point, not against others.

Learning Through Play

Chess should be fun, not a chore. I make sessions engaging by using puzzles, challenges, stories, and of course, actual games. For kids, I use game-based learning that feels like play. For adults, I make lessons practical and immediately applicable to real games. The best learning happens when students are enjoying themselves, so I work hard to keep every session interesting and enjoyable.

Continuous Progress Tracking

I track your progress systematically through regular game reviews, skill assessments, and rating improvements. You'll always know where you stand, what you've mastered, and what to work on next.
