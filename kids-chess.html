What Your Child Will Learn

Comprehensive chess education from basics to advanced concepts

Level 1: Beginner Basics

First 4-6 weeks

  • How to set up the chess board correctly
  • Movement of all pieces (Pawn, Knight, Bishop, Rook, Queen, King)
  • Special moves: Castling, En Passant, Promotion
  • Basic checkmate patterns (Queen + King, Rook + King)
  • Understanding check, checkmate, and stalemate
  • Playing complete games with confidence

Level 2: Fundamental Strategy

Weeks 7-12

  • Opening principles: control center, develop pieces, castle early
  • Basic tactical patterns: Forks, Pins, Skewers
  • Piece value and fair trades
  • Simple attacking techniques
  • Defending under pressure
  • Thinking ahead (planning 2-3 moves)

Level 3: Intermediate Skills

Months 4-6

  • Common opening systems (Italian Game, Scotch, etc.)
  • Advanced tactics: Discovered attacks, Deflection, Decoys
  • Middlegame planning and strategy
  • Pawn structure fundamentals
  • Basic endgame techniques
  • Time management in games

Level 4: Competitive Chess

6+ months

  • Deep tactical calculation
  • Positional understanding and evaluation
  • Opening repertoire development
  • Advanced endgame mastery
  • Tournament preparation and psychology
  • Game analysis and improvement methods

Common Questions from Parents

What age is best to start learning chess?

Children can start learning chess as early as 6 years old, though some bright 5-year-olds can begin too. The ideal learning age is 7-12 when children have developed enough concentration but are still highly receptive to new skills. However, teenagers (13-16) can also learn excellently and often progress faster due to better strategic understanding.

How long are the sessions for kids?

For younger children (6-8 years), I recommend 30-45 minute sessions as their attention span is shorter. For children 9 and above, full 1-hour sessions work well. I'm flexible and adjust session length based on your child's energy and engagement level.

Will my child get homework?

Yes, but it's designed to be fun and stress-free. I provide light chess homework to help children stay mentally active between sessions. These activities improve focus, memory, and problem-solving while keeping learning enjoyable. Homework typically takes 10-15 minutes and includes puzzles, simple games, or pattern practice.

Can chess really improve academic performance?

Absolutely. Multiple studies show chess students perform better academically, especially in mathematics, reading comprehension, and critical thinking. Chess develops concentration, memory, logical reasoning, and problem-solving skills that directly transfer to academic work. Many parents report improved grades and homework focus after their children start chess.

Is chess too difficult for my child to learn?

Not at all! When taught properly at an age-appropriate pace, chess is quite accessible for children. I break everything down into simple, understandable concepts and use visual, hands-on methods. Most children grasp the basics within 4-6 sessions and can play enjoyable games within 2 months.

What if my child loses interest or finds it boring?

I specialize in keeping kids engaged through stories, puzzles, challenges, and making learning feel like play. If a child seems bored, I immediately adjust my teaching approach. However, chess is inherently engaging for children when taught well, as it's like solving puzzles and playing strategy games, which kids naturally enjoy.

Should my child compete in tournaments?

Competition is optional and depends entirely on your child's interest and personality. Some kids love the challenge of tournaments while others prefer casual play. I can prepare interested students for school tournaments, but there's no pressure to compete. Chess benefits your child whether they compete or not.

How will I know about my child's progress?

I provide regular updates to parents about their child's development, what concepts we're covering, strengths I'm seeing, and areas we're working on. You're always welcome to ask questions or request updates. I believe in transparent communication with parents.

What makes your teaching different for kids?

I specialize in kids because I understand they need patience, encouragement, and age-appropriate teaching. I make chess fun while building real skills. My approach combines structured learning with playful engagement. I'm not just teaching chess moves, I'm developing your child's thinking abilities and confidence.
